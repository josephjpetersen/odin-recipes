Chocolate Moose

Description:

I hope you can excuse the fact, dear reader, that the above image is of a moose before preparation and not of the finished dish, the delectable Chocolate Moose. Unfortunately (or fortunately depending on who you ask), the final product's aroma was so mouthwatering it caused a stampede amongst my guests.

As our fellowship of feasters reached nigh Dionysian levels of voracious frenzy, I found my camera with all the "after" pictures being devoured by a young woman lost to the throes of gluttonous passion. Frankly, I wasn't even sure she was invited to dinner in the first place, and as I peered around the free-for-all, I noticed the number of attendees had doubled (with more pouring through the doors and windows every minute). The Chocolate Moose was soon gone down the gullets of the banqueteers ... and so were about half to two-thirds of my personal belongings

It was at this moment that I remembered my grandmother's old saying about this recipe: "Never make Chocolate Moose, you dolt!"

C'est la vie...

Ingredients:

  • 1 moose
  • 40 pounds of chocolate
  • 17 containers Cool Whip or equivalent
  • 1 cherry

Instructions:

  1. Send spouse to the wilderness to capture moose or have one delivered by UPS. Spouse can be substituted with any significant other or equivalent person willing to do your bidding.
  2. Meanwhile, melt chocolate in very large double boiler. Keep warm.
  3. Tie up moose with rope.
  4. Holding the moose by the tail, carefully dip in melted chocolate, covering it completely with a thin coating.
  5. Arrange moose attractively on large platter and refrigerate for 2 days to set chocolate.
  6. Remove rope, wash to remove chocolate (if necessary), and return rope to clothesline.
  7. Garnish chocolate moose with Cool Whip and top with a cherry.
  8. Serve immediately.
  9. If adventurous, chew the rope. Some say it is tastier.
  10. May be doubled for serving a crowd.
